COSC 326 Etude 11- Heads and Tails

Rhianne Price and Erina Jeffrey

Given three heads followed by three tails in a line, what is the minimum number of moves to alternate the heads and tails?

4 moves:

              H H H T T T

       H T H H        T T

              H T H H T T

       H T H T H        T

              H T H T H T

In our solution, we have assumed that “must always move two touching coins” means that you can move two or more touching coins, as moving more than touching coins is still moving two touching coins. We have also assumed that closing a gap counts as a move because “any movement of coins is a move”.

Try the same task with three heads followed by two tails.

3 moves:

       H H H T T

H T H H        T

       H T H H T

H T H T H

General Solution

In both problems we have used the same strategy, which is:

  1. Find the first occurrence of HHT, scanning from the left.
  2. Pick up the HT part from that HHT and move it to the front of the string.
  3. Close the gap left from moving that HT in step 2.
  4. Repeat until finished

This strategy can be generalised to any number of heads and tails where |heads - tails| < 2.

If there are more heads than tails, then our strategy has to be modified slightly. All the tails except the first are shifted to the front (which is a legal move under our moving more than two touching is moving two touching assumption), and then instead of looking for HHT’s we are now looking for TTH’s. Since we can use the same strategy to solve this for any number of coins where |heads - tails| < 2, it is a general one strategy.

This procedure scales linearly, as the number of moves is O(n). There is nothing in this strategy that guarantees it find will the minimum number of moves, but it just seems to work because there is no quicker way of making an HT or a TH than just plucking the first one out from the middle.
